The honest take: when CPO is NOT worth it

Here's what most buyers get wrong. CPO is excellent, but it isn't automatically the right call for everyone. If you're shopping a Subaru that's six or seven model years old, or one with high miles, it won't qualify for CPO anyway, so chasing the badge narrows your options for no reason. And if you tend to keep a car only two or three years and you're a low-mileage driver, you may never use enough of the extended powertrain coverage to justify the premium over a comparable clean-history used Subaru.

We'd steer a short-term, low-mile buyer toward a well-reconditioned non-CPO car and suggest putting the savings toward a larger down payment. CPO earns its keep for the buyer planning to drive the same Subaru well past 100,000 miles, that's where the 7-year/100,000-mile powertrain warranty stops being a sticker and starts being real money.

Subaru models on the used lot, and what to look for.

Because this is a Subaru store, the pre-owned Subaru rotation runs deeper here than at a non-franchise lot. Symmetrical all-wheel drive is standard across the gas lineup, which matters more than Vegas shoppers expect on Mount Charleston, Lee Canyon, and Brian Head winter runs.

  • Outback. The valley's default do-everything wagon. Higher ground clearance than a sedan, AWD standard, and a cabin that swallows ski gear and dog crates alike. On used examples, check that all scheduled CVT services were performed; we verify this in reconditioning, but ask to see the history on any car.
  • Forester. Boxy, upright, easy outward visibility, a favorite of valley parents and anyone who hauls bikes. The high seating position and big glass make it forgiving in I-15 traffic.
  • Crosstrek. The compact crossover for tighter budgets and tighter parking. Strong fuel economy for an AWD vehicle; check EPA estimates by exact model year and trim at the official EPA database before you assume the numbers.
  • Ascent. Three rows when you need them. Used Ascents move quickly with Summerlin and Henderson families, bring a short list, not a single VIN.
  • Legacy and Impreza. The sedan and hatchback value plays for commuters who still want all-wheel drive without the crossover ride height.

A note on EVs, because it comes up: the Subaru Solterra is built by Toyota, not Subaru, so its ownership and parts story differs from the gas lineup. Ask us directly if you're cross-shopping electric, and we'll give you the straight version rather than a brochure line.

Before you fall for a specific used car, two free habits we recommend to every shopper: run the VIN, and read the crash-test scores.

Run the VIN through NHTSA

Check for open recalls at NHTSA's free recall lookup (nhtsa.gov/recalls), it takes thirty seconds, and any reputable store will have already closed open recalls on its inventory. If we sell you a car, we verify recall status as part of reconditioning, but you should never take that on faith from anyone.

Read the crash-test scores

The IIHS (iihs.org) and NHTSA both publish ratings by model year. Subaru has historically performed well in IIHS testing, but scores vary by generation and trim, so confirm the exact year you're considering rather than assuming.

06The real number

What goes into your Nevada out-the-door number.

A used-car price online is not your out-the-door price, and we'd rather you hear that from us than feel surprised at the desk. In Nevada, expect the advertised price plus:

  • State and local sales tax. Clark County's combined sales-tax rate is 8.375% as of 2026; the Nevada Department of Motor Vehicles (dmv.nv.gov) is the authority on current rates and how vehicle tax is assessed.
  • Title and registration fees. Nevada charges a governmental services tax based on the vehicle's depreciated value, plus standard registration and title fees, all set by the Nevada DMV, not the dealership. The DMV's online registration-fee estimator (dmv.nv.gov) gives you a ballpark before you ever step in.
  • A documentation fee. A standard processing charge applies to the paperwork; ask for the exact figure up front and we'll give it to you in writing.

For independent pricing context on any specific year, trim, and mileage, we tell shoppers to cross-check Kelley Blue Book (kbb.com) or Edmunds (edmunds.com) values before they negotiate. Walking in with that homework done makes the whole conversation faster and more honest, and we mean that.

09Used & CPO questions

Frequently asked questions about used cars and Subaru CPO.

Does Findlay Subaru sell non-Subaru used cars?

Yes. The used inventory includes trade-ins across multiple makes alongside pre-owned Subarus.

The Subaru Certified Pre-Owned program, however, applies only to qualifying late-model Subaru vehicles that pass Subaru of America's 152-point factory inspection.

Is Findlay Subaru a legitimate place to buy a used car?

Yes, Findlay Subaru is an established Subaru dealership rated 4.7 stars from roughly 3,500-plus Google reviews, and it has been a BBB Accredited Business since June 7, 2016.

It's part of the Findlay Automotive Group, founded in 1971, which ranked #24 on Automotive News' 2025 Top 150 Dealership Groups.

What is a Subaru Certified Pre-Owned vehicle, and what warranty comes with it?

It's a low-mileage, late-model Subaru that has passed Subaru of America's 152-point factory inspection. Per Subaru's published CPO program (subaru.com), certification adds a 7-year/100,000-mile powertrain limited warranty from the original in-service date plus a 24-month/unlimited-mileage extension of the bumper-to-bumper terms, and the coverage is honored at any Subaru dealer nationwide.

Findlay Subaru is an authorized Subaru CPO retailer.

Should I always buy CPO instead of a regular used Subaru?

Not always. CPO is worth it if you plan to keep the car well past 100,000 miles and want factory-backed confidence.

If you drive few miles, keep cars only two or three years, or are shopping an older Subaru that wouldn't qualify anyway, a well-reconditioned non-CPO Subaru can be the smarter spend. We'll tell you honestly which lane fits your situation.

How do I check a used car's recall and safety history?

Run the VIN at nhtsa.gov/recalls for open recalls (it's free) and review crash-test ratings for the exact model year at iihs.org and nhtsa.gov.

We verify recall status as part of reconditioning, but you should never take that on faith from any seller.

What will my out-the-door price be in Las Vegas?

The advertised price plus Nevada sales tax (Clark County's combined rate is 8.375% as of 2026), the state's governmental services tax, registration and title fees set by the Nevada DMV (dmv.nv.gov), and a documentation fee.

Use the DMV's online fee estimator for a ballpark, and ask us for the doc fee in writing.

Can I bring my own mechanic before buying?

Absolutely. We welcome third-party pre-purchase inspections on used vehicles.

A store that discourages an outside look is one to be wary of.

Do you take trade-ins?

Yes. Findlay Subaru is a participating Subaru Trade Up Advantage dealer.

Bring your registration and any loan payoff details, and we'll apply your current vehicle's value toward a used or CPO Subaru with the numbers shown as separate lines.
